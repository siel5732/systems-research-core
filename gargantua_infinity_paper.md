## 2. Mathematical & Relativistic Formulation

### 2.1 The Schwarzschild Metric and Radial Mapping
We map our 1D lattice of $N = 4,096$ sites to a circular orbit of varying radial distance $r(n)$ around Gargantua. The event horizon is located at the Schwarzschild radius $r_s = 2.0$. To avoid a mathematical division by zero at the singularity, we introduce a near-horizon cushion $\delta = 0.05$.

The radial distance $r(n)$ of each lattice site $n \in [0, N-1]$ is mapped as:

$$r(n) = r_s + \delta + (r_{max} - r_s - \delta) \sin^2\left(\frac{\pi n}{N}\right)$$

where $r_{max} = 10.0$ represents flat space far from the black hole. By design, $r(n)$ is closest to the event horizon at $n = 0$ and $n = N-1$ ($r \approx 2.05$), and furthest in flat space at $n = N/2 = 2048$ ($r = 10.0$).

### 2.2 Gravitational Time Dilation and Warped Hopping
In general relativity, the Schwarzschild lapse function $\alpha(r)$ represents the rate of flow of coordinate time relative to proper time:

$$\alpha(r) = \sqrt{1 - \frac{r_s}{r}}$$

Near the event horizon ($r \to r_s$), $\alpha(r) \to 0$, causing coordinate time to freeze. We map this physical time dilation directly onto the quantum hopping integrals $t_n$ between adjacent sites:

$$t_n = -t_0 \alpha(r(n)) = -t_0 \sqrt{1 - \frac{r_s}{r(n)}}$$

Where $t_0 = 1.0$ is the flat-space hopping rate. Thus, as the quantum state moves closer to the event horizon ($n \to 0$ or $n \to N$), its ability to "hop" or propagate is highly suppressed by gravitational time dilation, mirroring the relativistic slowing of coordinate time.

## 4. Hamiltonian & Unitary Evolution in 12-Qubit State Space
The tight-binding Schwarzschild-Cantor Hamiltonian on our 12-qubit ($4,096$-dimensional) Hilbert space is defined as:

$$H = \sum_{n=0}^{N-1} V_n |n\rangle \langle n| + \sum_{n=0}^{N-1} t_n \left( |n\rangle \langle n+1| + |n+1\rangle \langle n| \right)$$

with periodic boundary conditions $|N\rangle \equiv |0\rangle$.

Because of the massive $4,096 \times 4096$ scale, numerical approximations of unitary time evolution would accumulate significant error. Instead, we compute the exact spectral decomposition of $H$ using LAPACK diagonalization:

$$H = \sum_{k=0}^{N-1} E_k |\phi_k\rangle \langle \phi_k|$$

This allows us to evaluate the exact time-evolved state $|\Psi(t)\rangle$ at coordinate time $t$ with zero error accumulation:

$$|\Psi(t)\rangle = e^{-i H t} |\Psi(0)\rangle = \sum_{k=0}^{N-1} e^{-i E_k t} \langle \phi_k | \Psi(0) \rangle |\phi_k\rangle$$

The system is initialized as a Gaussian wave packet spawned in flat space ($n = 2048$) with an inward momentum $k_0 = -0.15$ propelling it toward Gargantua:

$$\Psi_n(0) \propto \exp\left( -\frac{(n - 2048)^2}{2\sigma^2} \right) \exp(i k_0 n)$$
